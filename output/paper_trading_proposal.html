2. The Strategy: Regime-Adaptive Credit Spreads + Iron Condors

How It Works (Plain English)

The system reads the market's "mood" (bull, bear, or sideways) using an 80-day moving average, then picks the right play:

  • Bull market → Sell bull put credit spreads (bet the market won't crash)
  • Bear market → Sell bear call credit spreads (bet the market won't rally)
  • Sideways / high IV → Sell iron condors (bet the market stays in a range)

4. Safety Infrastructure (Already Built)

The paper trader has extensive safety rails. These are not being changed:

🛑 Kill Switch

Instant halt of all trading via Telegram command or DB flag. No new trades opened, existing positions monitored only.

📉 Drawdown Circuit Breaker

If portfolio drawdown exceeds 40% peak-to-trough, all new entries are blocked. Auto-resets when equity recovers above threshold.

🔥 Portfolio Heat Cap

Maximum 60% of portfolio at risk simultaneously. Prevents over-concentration in correlated positions.

📝 Write-Ahead Logging

Every trade intent is logged before execution. Crash recovery reconstructs state from log.

NEW in fix/alignment-patches: Drawdown CB persistence across restarts, IC partial close retry with CRITICAL escalation, paper_mode validation (blocks live API URLs).

Key Divergence Signals (Paper vs Backtest)

These are the things that will tell us if the backtest was honest:

  1. Fill quality: Are we getting the credits the backtester assumed? If real credits are 20%+ lower, the backtest was optimistic.
  2. Trade frequency: Backtest averages ~41 trades/year (~3.4/month). If paper trading generates significantly more or fewer, the signal logic diverges.
  3. Regime detection: Does the live regime detector agree with what the backtester would have said? Log both for comparison.
  4. Slippage impact: The backtester added slippage estimates. Real slippage may differ.

6. Kill Criteria — When to Stop

🔴 HARD STOP — Immediately halt paper trading if:

  • Drawdown exceeds 30% (circuit breaker should catch at 40%, but manual override at 30%)
  • More than 5 consecutive losses (backtest max streak was 3)
  • Fill prices consistently 40%+ worse than backtest assumptions
  • System enters trades the backtest wouldn't have (regime mismatch)
  • Any execution error that places unintended orders

🟡 PAUSE & REVIEW — If after 4 weeks:

  • Win rate below 60% (vs 81% expected)
  • Annualized return tracking below 10% (vs 32.7% expected — accounting for walk-forward discount)
  • Trade count is 50%+ off from backtest expectations
  • Significant unexplained divergence between paper and backtest results

🟢 SUCCESS CRITERIA — After 8 weeks:

  • Win rate >70%
  • Positive cumulative P&L
  • Max drawdown <20%
  • Trade execution aligns with backtest expectations (±30%)
  • No system errors or unintended behavior
  • → Ready to discuss live trading with real capital
